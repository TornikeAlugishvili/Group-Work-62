Pizza is a flat baked bread that is usually topped with tomato sauce,
cheese (mozzarella), and various other ingredients. Pizza is part of Italian, specifically
Neapolitan cuisine, which has today spread widely across many cultures.
Specialized establishments where pizza is baked and/or sold are called pizzerias.


History

It is known that the ancient Greeks baked bread with various toppings, including olives,
herbs, and cheese. In ancient Rome, a type of bread called *placenta* was known,
which was also made with cheese. Modern pizza originated in Naples.
